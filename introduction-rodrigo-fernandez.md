# Introduction — Rodrigo Fernandez

I'm Rodrigo Fernandez, based in Montevideo, Uruguay.

My work at DualBoot Partners has moved across the full stack. I started as a frontend developer, then backend, then took on technical lead roles. From there I led teams — as small as 5 people, as large as 20 — across different projects and clients.

That experience led me into the Head of Engineering role for our Latin America offices, where the work shifted from writing code to managing capacity, delivery, and coordination across multiple offices.

Today I work as a Solutions Architect. I design systems end to end: architecture decisions, technology selection, integration patterns, and the tradeoffs behind each one.

One recent project puts this to work directly. A client needed to migrate off Microsoft Dynamics into custom-built software. The hard part wasn't the code — it was that most of the business logic lived inside Dynamics and wasn't cleanly extractable. We built a modular monolith: modules isolated cleanly enough to split into microservices later, without taking on that complexity up front. The team started at four people. It's now 20 to 25, actively building.

That kind of extraction — pulling business logic out of a legacy system nobody fully documented — used to take years. With AI folded into our methodology, we're now doing it up to 90% faster than a couple of years ago. That changes what's realistic for a modernization timeline.

So before I get into what we'd propose, I want to understand your side first: what does your current system actually do that isn't written down anywhere? Where does the undocumented logic live — in a person, in a database trigger, in a batch job nobody's touched in years? That's usually where the real project is.
